Frequently Asked

Common Questions, Honest Answers

Everything you might want to know before booking your first consultation.

What conditions does Dr. Krithishree treat? expand_more
Dr. Krithishree treats the full spectrum of adult and child psychiatric conditions — depression, anxiety, OCD, schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, sleep disorders, addiction, psychosexual concerns, paranoia and behavioural issues in children. See the full list.
How long is a typical consultation? expand_more
First consultations are typically 45–60 minutes to fully understand your history and concerns. Follow-up visits run 20–30 minutes. Nothing is rushed — the time is yours.
Is my information kept confidential? expand_more
Absolutely. Every conversation, record and detail is strictly confidential, in line with medical ethics and Indian medical council regulations. Your identity is never disclosed outside the clinic.
Do I need a referral to book an appointment? expand_more
No referral is required. You can directly call +91 99000 70209 or fill the appointment form on the contact page. If you have past medical records or prescriptions, please bring them along.
Are online or phone consultations available? expand_more
Yes — and Dr. Krithishree is a genuine advocate of it. As she has written on e-mental health, psychiatric care is particularly well suited to remote consultation, since physical examination is less central than in other specialties — and it helps extend care to people who cannot easily reach a clinic. Phone and video consultations are available for follow-ups; the first consultation is generally preferred in person where possible.
Will I be put on medication straight away? expand_more
Not necessarily. Across her published writing, Dr. Krithishree consistently frames treatment as a combination — psychotherapy, lifestyle change and, where it genuinely helps, medication. Many concerns improve with therapy, better sleep and routine, and supportive counselling alone. Any prescription is explained openly, including side effects and alternatives.
